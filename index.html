Ruijuan Li

I am currently a postdoctoral Researcher in Maloof lab, University of California, Davis, under the supervison of Dr. Julin Maloof and Dr. Richard Michelmore. My work focuses on using advanced genomics, statistical, and computational methods to accelerate breeding of the important crop plant rapeseed (Brassica napus). My PhD dissertation work was to explore the gene expression and small RNA expression reprogramming of cotton in response to nematode infestation, and to use system biology method to understand how gene regulatory networks of cotton were modified under the attack of nematode. I also did a postdoctoral training in USDA-ARS under the supervision of Dr. John Erpedling where I used GBS data of cotton for GWAS, and identified several QTL and candidate genes associated with nematode resistance.

I would like to pursue my career in population genetics, quantitative genetics, and spend my time using data analytics method to help understand and ultimately engineer biological systems.
